    description

    X.O. cognac is an official, legally enforced grade of cognac. XO stands for “Extra Old” and indicates that the brandies in the product are at least six years old (although the average may be much older than that). Unfortunately, while the XO designation does presumably guarantee a fact about the particular brandy, it should not be presumed to impart other objective or subjective qualities to a product. This distinction is of primary interest for degustation. For mixed drinks purposes, any cognac will usually do.

    flavors
    brandy, wood
    substitute
    In punches and some mixed drinks where the subtler details of cognac are overshadowed by other ingredients, it can be possible—with careful discretion—to substitute other dry-tasting aged grape brandies, including inexpensive ones.
